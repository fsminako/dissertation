                    Just click on the icons to get to the download page. oal miner PT Indo Tambangraya Megah booked US$131 million net profit last year, a 107 percent increase from $63 million in 2015, thanks to a coal-price increase to $59.8 per ton in the fourth quarter from $49.9 per ton in third quarter of 2016. “This was the highest increase since 2013, which was caused by limited stock in China and global bad weather,” Indo Tambangraya president director Kirana Limpaphayom said in a statement on Wednesday in Jakarta. Indo Tambangraya data showed last year that China had regained its position as the biggest buyer of coal from the company with 6.7 million tons after a significant drop in 2015 to 4.4 million tons from 6.8 million tons in 2014. Japan bought 5.2 million tons from Indo Tambangraya. However, India, which was the largest buyer in 2015 with 5.4 million tons, in 2016 only bought 2.8 million tons.  Meanwhile, the domestic market made up 3.7 million tons of sales, the same figure as the previous year, (Read also: Coal-loving Indonesian investor doubles down after 39% gain) Indo Tambangraya finance director Yulius Gozali said the miner aimed to sell 27 million tons of coal this year, compared to 26.7 million tons in 2016. He said there would be a production decrease at two of its subsidiaries, Indominco and Trubaindo, respectively 0.2 and 0.8 million tons in anticipation of the rainy season. He said the company had allocated $60.3 million in capital expenditure, mostly for the procurement of machinery and infrastructure development at its subsidiaries.  (dra/bbn) © 2016 - 2024 PT.
