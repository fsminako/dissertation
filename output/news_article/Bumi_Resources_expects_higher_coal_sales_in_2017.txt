The Bakrie family-controlled company estimates its coal sales reached 87.7 million tons last year, which would mark an increase of 10.6 percent over 2015, according to the firm's statement. As much as 60 percent of [this year's] sales volume had been agreed and it was hoped that that figure could increase to 75 percent by the end of the first quarter through the finalization of several annual contracts with Japanese counterparts, Bumi Resources director and corporate secretary Dileep Srivastava wrote in the statement. (Read also: Bumi refuels certainty with bankruptcy shield plea) The company expects coal prices to rise by at least 30 percent this year from an average of US$42.1 per ton last year.
